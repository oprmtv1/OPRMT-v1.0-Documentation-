1. Purpose

The OPRMT™ Community exists to create and maintain a high-quality, open, and globally recognized prompt engineering standard.
To achieve this, contributors must interact in a way that supports:

Respect

Clarity

Professionalism

Technical rigor

Inclusivity

Productive collaboration


This Code of Conduct applies to all online and offline spaces related to OPRMT™.


---

2. Expected Behavior

All contributors—whether submitting issues, editing documentation, joining discussions, or creating tools—are expected to demonstrate:

2.1 Respectful Communication

Speak and write professionally.

Assume good intent unless proven otherwise.

Give constructive feedback, not personal criticism.


2.2 Technical Integrity

Base arguments on evidence, examples, or data.

Maintain clarity and accuracy when discussing the specification.

Follow the OPRMT™ structure and terminology in contributions.


2.3 Collaboration Over Conflict

Work toward shared understanding.

Avoid unproductive debates or gatekeeping.

Help newcomers and maintain a supportive environment.


2.4 Inclusivity

All skill levels are welcome.

Contributors from any background may propose improvements.


2.5 Accountability

Admit mistakes early.

Correct errors promptly.

Respect review decisions and governance rules.



---

3. Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are prohibited:

3.1 Harassment

Insults, slurs, or derogatory comments

Personal attacks or intimidation

Persistent, aggressive, or bad-faith argumentation


3.2 Disruptive Behavior

Spamming

Hijacking threads

Repetitive or off-topic posting

Attempting to derail discussions


3.3 Abuse of the Contribution Process

Submitting content intentionally violating OPRMT™ rules

Disregarding Maintainer review feedback

Submitting malicious code, prompts, or tooling

Misrepresenting affiliation or credentials


3.4 Trademark or Brand Misuse

Any attempt to misuse, misrepresent, or dilute the OPRMT™ brand, the Open Prompt Standard, or ImperialTurf LLC’s trademarks is considered a serious violation.


---

4. Reporting Violations

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, report it immediately:

support@oprmt.com

Include:

A description of the incident

Any relevant links

Any evidence (screenshots, timestamps, etc.)


Reports are handled confidentially.


---

5. Enforcement

5.1 Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers must enforce the Code of Conduct fairly and consistently.
They may take one or more of the following actions:

Verbal/written warning

Temporary ban from discussions

Rejection or closure of contributions

Restriction of repository access

Permanent ban for severe or repeat violations


5.2 Oversight Authority

ImperialTurf LLC retains final authority over:

Removal of contributors

Removal of maintainers

Arbitration of disputes

Enforcement in community spaces


Decisions by ImperialTurf LLC are final.


---

6. Community Membership Requirement

As stated in CONTRIBUTING.md:

Anyone may propose changes or submit issues

Only verified members of the OPRMT™ Community may receive approvals or merges


Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in immediate suspension or termination of membership.


---

7. Amendments

This Code of Conduct may be updated as the community grows.
All changes require:

Maintainer majority approval

Oversight confirmation

Update in CHANGELOG.md
